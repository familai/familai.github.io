Mortgage Rates Plateau After Weeks of Declines

Good morning, homeowners!

After six weeks of declining rates, the mortgage market is taking a breather. The 30-year fixed mortgage rate sits at 6.21% according to Freddie Mac, while our daily survey shows 6.24%, virtually unchanged from yesterday. The 10-Year Treasury yield, a key indicator, is also relatively stable at 4.169%.

News outlets are reporting a mixed bag. While some lenders are still advertising rates in the 5% range, the overall trend suggests that the recent downward momentum may have stalled. This aligns with reports of a cooling housing market where sellers are retreating, giving buyers more negotiation power.

Compared to last week, the narrative has shifted slightly. We saw reports of improving affordability and stable mortgage rates benefiting buyers and refinancers. Now, the focus is on the stabilization of rates and the increasing leverage of buyers. The previously reported uptick in November home sales has seemingly had little impact on today's rates.

The Outlook: It's a wait-and-see game. With inflation still present (CPI at 325.031) and the Federal Funds Rate at 3.88%, further significant declines in mortgage rates may be limited in the short term. However, a cooling market and shifting buyer-seller dynamics could keep rates from rising dramatically. Stay tuned for further updates!
